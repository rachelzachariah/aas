Sep29, 2016

Arithmetic and Quantum Chaos

11:00 AM - 12:00 PM, Thursday Sep. 29, 901 Van Vleck

The distribution of eigenvalues and behavior of eigenfunctions of the Laplace-Beltrami operator for a smooth compact Riemannian manifold are central problems in the field of Quantum Chaos. For arithmetic surfaces such as torus and modular surface more can be proved by taking advantage of the extra arithmetic structure. In this talk I will describe these problems and mention some results in arithmetic settings which are (in part) proved using number theory.

Nadya Markin

Nov10, 2016

Fast decodabilty bounds of space-time codes.

11:15 AM - 12:15 PM, Thursday Nov. 10, 901 Van Vleck

Space-time codes are an algebraic tool used in wireless communication to achieve high data rate and reliability. We review the construction of codes via arepresentation of division algebras and show how number theoretic criteria can be used to meet basic design criteria for space-time codes. Decoding complexity of these codes is related to finding families of complex matrices satisfying the mutual orthogonality condition AB*+BA*=0. We will go over some results on the bounds of the decoding complexity of space-time codes using skew-Hermitian matrices.

Alisha Zachariah

Nov17, 2016

Low Complexity Channel Estimation

11:00 AM - 12:00 PM, Thursday Nov. 17, 901 Van Vleck

Several forms of wireless communication (e.g. RADAR) involve estimating the channel through which signals are sent. To achieve high resolution estimation, the number of time samples of a signal may be restrictively large. In this talk I will describe a channel model and a connection to rich mathematical structure in representations of the Heisenberg group that suggests a channel estimation algorithm with arithmetic complexity sub-linear in number of samples.

Kejia Wang

Dec8, 2016

A journey to low spherical cap discrepancy.

11:00 AM - 12:00 PM, Thursday Dec. 8, 901 Van Vleck

Discrepancy is a measurement of how uniform a point distribution is. The lower the discrepancy, the more uniform the distribution is. In the unit cube, [0,1]^d, the notion of low discrepancy is well studied, and low discrepancy sequences are well understood. In recent years, this field has been enriched with sophisticated sequence construction techniques using arithmetic curves over finite fields, know as the Niederreiter-Xing method. However, the spherical discrepancy on the unit sphere S^2 remains largely unexplored. In fact, the definition of low spherical discrepancy is not even officially established. Most ``well-spaced" spherical sequences found in literature are obtained by lifting well-spaced sequences form [0,1]^2 to the sphere via certain maps (for example, the Lambert Transformation). In this talk, we will investigate direct sequence construction algorithms on the sphere and the related spherical cap discrepancy.
